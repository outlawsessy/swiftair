Chicago's Greatest Things To Do
Save This Article

Chicago is a grand, vibrant city and sprawling metropolitan area. It is the home of the blues and the truth of jazz, the heart of comedy and the first builder of the skyscraper. It is one of the world's great cities, and yet the metropolitan luxuries of theater, shopping, and fine dining have barely put a dent in real Midwestern friendliness. It's a city with a swagger, but without the surliness or fakery found in cities of like size.

Chicago’s picturesque skyline calls across the waters of huge Lake Michigan, a majestic curtain that soon reveals world-class museums of art and science, miles of sandy beaches, huge parks and public art, and perhaps the finest downtown collection of architecture in the world.

While the city has many attractions — both world-class and regionally organic — in its huge central/downtown area, lots of Chicagoans live and play outside of the central business district. To understand more of the city, travelers can venture away from the Loop and Michigan Avenue into its vibrant neighborhoods to soak up the local nightlife, sample the wide range of hearty dining options, and enjoy family-friendly stops rich with local meaning. Thanks to the city's massive public transit system, all parts of Chicago are accessible.

With a wealth of iconic sights and neighborhoods to explore, there's enough to fill a visit of weeks or even months without ever seeing the end. Prepare to cover a lot of ground: the meaning of Chicago is only found in movement, through its subways and archaic elevated tracks, in the pride of tired feet and eyes raised once more to the sky.
